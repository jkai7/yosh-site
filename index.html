Francisco A. Larios

Is a Florida-licensed attorney currently working as an associate at Coccia De Angelis Vecchio & Associati, where he advises sports clients and represents them in disputes before sports governing bodies, such as FIFA and FIBA, and international arbitral tribunals, such as the Basketball Arbitral Tribunal (BAT) and the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS). Mr. Larios has particular experience representing players, agents and clubs before the BAT in contractual disputes related to employment, agency fees, and image rights. He is also regularly appointed as ad hoc clerk in CAS proceedings.

Francisco earned a Juris Doctor degree with honors

The Court of Arbitration for Sport dismisses the Appeal of Al Hilal Saudi Club against the Asian Football Confederation and Al Ahli Club Dubai, 11 Nov. 2015 Mr. Larios succesfully represented the Al Ahli Club Dubai (United Arab Emirates) in an expedited proceeding before the Court of Arbitraiton for Sport (CAS). The CAS dismissed the appeal filed by Al Hilal Saudi Club (Saudi Arabia) and confirmed the UAE club remains qualified for the 2015 AFC Champions League Final against Guangzhou Evergrande. The Court of Arbitration for Sport rejects the appeal filed by the Football Association of Serbia and upholds in part the appeal filed by the Football Association of Albania, 10 July 2015 Mr. Larios acted as ad hoc clerk in two appeal arbitration proceedings arising from the UEFA European Championship qualifying match held on 14 October 2014 in Belgrade between the Serbian and Albanian senior national teams. The Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS) dismissed the appeal filed by the Football Association of Serbia (FAS) and upheld in part the appeal filed by the Football Association of Albania (FAA), deeming the match forfeited by the FAS by the score of 0:3.
